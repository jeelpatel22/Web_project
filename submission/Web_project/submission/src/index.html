Zebra Jumping Spider

The Zebra Spider (Salticus scenicus), also known as the Zebra Jumping Spider or Salticidae, is a small and agile arachnid found in various parts of North America, including the United States and Canada. It belongs to the Salticidae family, which is known for its excellent jumping ability and unique hunting techniques.

Male zebra spiders are 5–6 mm long, compared to the females' mm length.The chelicerae of male zebra spiders are noticeably larger than those of females. Although the anterior and posterior lateral eyes (ALE, PLE) are also huge in comparison to the extremely small posterior median eyes (PME), the anterior and posterior median eyes (AME) are particularly enlarged in spiders of the Salticidae family. It has eight eyes altogether, with the exceptional binocular vision being mostly attributed to the unusually big anterior median eyes. These little spiders have white stripes on their black bodies.

The reproduction in the zebra spider

In the spring and summer, this species reproduces.A courtship dance is performed by the male spider when it first encounters a female. They move their pedipalps, front legs, and abdomen up and down while dancing.Males communicate their desire to mate with females by flashing their striped markings during the courtship dance. The ritual of courtship strongly depends on vision. The likelihood that the female will wish to mate increases with improved dancing. Males should exercise caution when approaching females since they run the risk of being attacked or thought to be a prey species. The female will allow the man to approach if she is impressed by his dance.

how they hunt

When the angle occupied by the prey by their lateral eyes surpasses 5.5°, they turn towards the prey. Only moving objects evoke orientation; the prey's velocity plays no role in determining reaction distance. Size and speed of the prey affect the likelihood that stalking will occur after orienting. As the zebra spider approaches its (stationary) victim, its stalk velocity gradually decreases.
